Bitly Product Offsite · May 18–20, Denver

Before you arrive

A few hours of prep will make the difference between a great offsite and a great conversation that goes nowhere.

What we're here to do

We're not here to review roadmaps or revisit quarterly planning. We're here to make hard strategic choices about where Bitly plays to win over the next 2–3 years.

That means arriving with a clear-eyed view of our customers, our evidence, and our honest assessment of where we have a real right to win — not just where we'd like to compete.

Everyone

Read before arriving

Market context brief — shared by EOD Friday. Read it in full.
~15 min. Focus on competitive moves and customer behavior shifts.
Come with your biggest strategic question — the one thing you most want this offsite to answer. Write it down.
You'll share it in the Welcome session.
Know the decision model — CPO makes final prioritization calls. Come ready to advocate hard, then commit to the outcome.
Product managers (8)

Prepare your lightning talk

Prepare an 8–9 min talk using the template below. No slides required, but bring a visual if it helps.
One sharp insight beats five hedged ones.
Bring at least one direct customer quote — something a real customer said about the problem you're highlighting.
Not a paraphrase. A real quote anchors the room.
Know your evidence — be ready for "how do you know this is real?"
Lightning talk template (8–9 min)
1
Customer segment
Who specifically? Size, role, context. 1 min.
2
Top 1–2 problems
Painful, specific, evidenced. Quote a customer. 3 min.
3
Evidence
Data, research, calls, NPS patterns. 2 min.
4
Biggest opportunity
If we solved this well, what becomes possible? 2 min.
Lead designer (1)

Bring the customer experience lens

Prepare 1–2 customer journey sketches for segments you know best. Rough is fine.
These anchor Day 1 customer lens sharpening. Print or bring a tablet.
Set up a Figjam board (or bring stickies) — you'll facilitate the Day 1 synthesis affinity mapping.
During Day 2 breakouts, your job is rapid concept sketching — 2–3 rough screens per bet, not polished design.
Product leaders + CPO + strategy (4)

Come with your strategic frame

Identify your biggest strategic question — the thing you genuinely don't know. Share it in Welcome.
Be ready to play devil's advocate on every bet — especially the ones you're most excited about.
Your job in structured debate is to push, not to validate.
Know your walk-away criteria — what makes a bet a clear yes or no for you? Being explicit reduces the feeling that decisions are arbitrary.

Norms for the room

Do
Push hard on ideas, not people. "I'm not convinced by the evidence" not "I don't believe you."
Don't
Sand down your view to avoid conflict. Politeness that obscures real disagreement kills good decisions.
Do
Advocate strongly, then commit fully. Once we decide, we row together.
Don't
Re-litigate decisions after they're made. Bring disagreement into the room, not the hallway.
Do
Use "yes, and" during idea generation. Critique mode comes in structured debate, not breakouts.
Don't
Anchor to the roadmap. We're making new bets, not defending existing plans.
